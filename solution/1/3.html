Solution: Learn to Play

Written by Chris Jones

There are a few things that can be noticed when looking at this puzzle:

  • The amount of actual content is extremely minimal.
  • The formatting of the words seems very particular. However, it changes whenever the page is resized (suggesting we probably don’t want to draw lines between words or use their absolute positions).
  • Some of the words are associated with puzzlehunts, e.g. “puzzled”, “hints”, “round”, and in fact more of them are very close to (off by one letter from) words associated with puzzlehunts, e.g. “older” vs. “order”, “joints” vs. “points”, “tram”/”beams” vs. “team(s)”, and “salved” vs “solved”.

What the first and second suggest is we need to look elsewhere for additional information, and we need to discover the origin of these words and the layout this puzzle is mimicking. Combining the third point with the title, if we want to “learn to play puzzleball”, the logical leap to make is to look at the Rules page for the Galactic Puzzle Hunt.

Comparing with the Rules page, many of the words in the puzzle are excerpted from the page (at the same locations as on the page), with one letter changed in each word. Taking the letter from the original page and reading down, we get the message USERFREEAGENT.

This is progress, but it’s not everything. There are still 14 words not found in the Rules. The remaining words are instead excerpted from the hunt’s FAQ page, another place you might “learn to play”. Matching up words again, the changed letters from the original page spell out PWTOPOFTHETEAM when read down.

These are credentials for logging in to the website as the user “freeagent”, with password “topoftheteam”. Once logged in, you will see this team is fairly uninteresting: it cannot submit guesses or hints and does not appear on the leaderboard. The final discovery to make is that the Rules and FAQ pages displayed for this team contain many changed letters. Taking letters from the original pages again, with each page supplying one word we find the solution FIREARMS REGULATION, which is conceivably one important rule for any puzzlehunt.

Author's Notes

If you opened up the HTML for the puzzle page, you may have shaken your head in disgust. Because text is displayed differently on different browsers, we had a hard time coming up with a way for the puzzle page to exactly match the Rules/FAQ pages, without actually including the text of the Rules/FAQ in the HTML of the puzzle (where a tech-savvy team might skip the major insight of the puzzle). Ultimately, the note recommending Google Chrome was included because this is the environment in which the text was measured, and where the text should be sufficiently aligned to convey the mechanic of the puzzle.
