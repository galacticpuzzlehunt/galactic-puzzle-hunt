The leftover words give the message you are the path finder now notice who is with you. Each of these songs is either a solo or has a principal singer who sings the indicated lyrics. These characters are listed below in the final table.

Now we can tackle the note. The note gives a clear ordering and enumerations for something. Each step in the note contains a reference to a specific ability of a Core Pathfinder character class, as noted by “finding a path” repeated in the clue phrase and the flavor text. (A few of the phrases include further clues toward the class because their abilities are bit more generic.)

First, place your blood on the lock. We need to make sure your bloodline is powerful enough. Sorcerer Bloodline
Second, rage at the silliness of these instructions. Barbarian Rage
Third, meditate, focus your ki, and kick the chest. Monk Ki Pool
Fourth, inspire courage among your companions by giving a shout. Bard Inspire Courage
Fifth, recite your spelling list like you had in school. Wizard Arcane School
Sixth, sneakily attack the lock with a pick to open a secret compartment. Rogue Sneak Attack
Seventh, switch the wires to channel energy to the proper conduits. Cleric Channel Energy
Eighth, now your gloves are probably in poor shape. Change them. Druid Shapechange
Ninth, specialize in fighting with the absurdity of these directions. Fighter Weapon Specialization
Tenth, learn that your favorite enemy is “confusing instructions”. Ranger Favored Enemy
Last, lay your hands on the chest, pray, and open it. Paladin Lay On Hands

Each of the characters can be fairly unambiguously matched to these classes. Indexing into the character name by the given number gives the phrase FF MP RESTORE. Since the flavor text indicates we’re looking for an item, we find the item in the Final Fantasy series of games that restores magic points, which is the ETHER.

Author's Notes

This puzzle was inspired by the song "It's Hard to Be the Bard" from Something Rotten! Sounds like something the Bard would sing in the tavern after an adventure, no?

It should be noted that since Pathfinder is based on Dungeons and Dragons, familiarity with the latter is enough to note the class matching. With the exception of Sorcerer and Wizard, all of the listed abilities exist in D&D 3.5.

During the hunt, we saw that people were having significant difficulty interpreting the cluephrase. Since this wasn't meant to be the difficult or interesting step of the puzzle, we decided to release the cluephrase's enumeration. There was also a lot of ambiguity in whether to use "William Shakespeare", "Shakespeare", or even "The Bard". I would like to apologize for any difficulty the current form of the puzzle caused.
