Solution: The Superbowl

Written by Lewis Chen

Some of the sentences contain three definitions: two of the words, when concatenated, make the third, albeit in a way that is linguistically unfounded. For example, [obvious] = OVERT, [cutting] = AXING, which combines to form [too great of a demand] = OVERTAXING, but OVERTAXING is actually formed from OVER+TAXING. Thus we can extract the letter T, as it shifts from one side of the compound word to the other. Indeed, the title also has this property: SUPERBOWL = SUPERB + OWL. This is indeed a joke on Reddit as /r/superbowl has pictures of superb owls.

The “combined” definition may come before or after the two parts, but the two parts are always correctly ordered. So, for instance, if A = B+C, then the definitions will either come in the order of A, B, C or B, C, A.

Thus, extracting the letters, we get the answer: ASSOCIATED PRESS. One could say that shifting letters from one side to the other could demonstrate associativity of “pressing” words together. (e.g. (OVER+T)+AXING = OVERT+AXING = OVERTAXING = OVER+TAXING = OVER+(T+AXING))

Author's Notes

I wrote this puzzle on Super Bowl day, February 5th. It was a silly idea spurred from earlier discussion of the Superb Owl. And thus it became a puzzle. At that point I had already written (or at least had concrete plans for) two very difficult puzzles (and on top of that a pretty hard metapuzzle as well), so this was aimed to be an easier puzzle for a change of pace.

There are a rather limited number of words that are available, hence the somewhat worse pairs. I also hoped that all of the words could be compound words but alas had to settle for some prefix/suffix pairs (e.g. IMP+ISH and EN+DANGER). LOGOUT having UT as a word is also a little unfortunate.

Originally I thought of using a sentence to perhaps clue two letters (e.g. ca(retakers) cluing RE) but RE was the only pair that I could feasibly express this way, and thus that would be an obvious inconsistency that would’ve only been fine if I could express another one or two pairs. I also noted that I could come up with a (somewhat ridiculous) overarching narrative, which is perhaps a bit more interesting of a presentation than giving bare pairs of definitions. Finally, the combined word got its own definition as a confirmation check that you got the right words. Hence every letter got its own sentence.

I’m actually pretty happy that ASSOCIATED PRESS was one of the three answers remaining at this stage of the hunt development. It is surprisingly more fitting than I could’ve ever hoped for. I only realized how fitting it was after writing the puzzle.
